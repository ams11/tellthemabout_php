Why?
That's a difficult question... without really very much of a good answer. Traveling around the world in planes and cruise ships and having pre-arranged hotel rooms all along the way just isn't an exciting enough way to do it! A much better way is to take a beat-up old car and attempt to drive it 60,000 miles on five continents with only a vague semblance of a plan as to how it's all going to go!

The inspiration
  • The latest incarnation of the Bull Run, brought to you by SpikeTV raced about 2500 miles across North America... on highways! Exciting, but un-impressive!
  • The Dakar Rally traverses Northern Africa, crossing the Sahara desert. Certainly ambitious, but the teams regularly spend over $100K on their cars - that almost makes their performances predictable!
  • The Mongol Rally drives a mere 12,000 miles from London to Mongolia. Some of the roads are even paved!
  • The Pan-American highway is a 30,000 mile track across the entirety of North and South America. Admittedly impressive, but there's five other continents out there!
  • The Long Way Round explored a path around the globe on a pair of motorcycles. Getting interesting, but they did have an entire support crew!
  • The Road of Bones (a.k.a. the Kolyma Highway) was part of the Long Way Round, and the trek across Siberia is generally considered the single worst piece of road on this planet. Sounds like a challenge!
    •

    The only [obvious] conclusion?
     Combine the routes of all of the above for an epic 60,000 mile journey around the globe! Since we clearly lack the resources of the Long Way Round or the Dakar Rally, do it in a Mongol Rally style car - a 25 year-old piece of junk that shouldn't be allowed to travel six thousand miles on its own let alone sixty thousand! Just to round out the adventure, throw in a few side trips: the RickShaw Run, a drive across Australia, and a trip to Antarctica for good measure!

    The details
    If the Mongol Rally is any indication, even trying to stick to a particular route is futile undertaking. However, this is the current plan. Start in Moscow, Russia and finish back home in Seattle, WA in the United States about a year later.

    The charities
    With the Mongol Rally and the Rickshaw Run as the inspiration (and integral parts of the journey), we would also like to turn this into not just an adventure for ourselves, but a way of raising money for some great charities too! Browse the charity page for more details.
